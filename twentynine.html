Question 290

Imagine you're designing a visually appealing Power BI report to present sales data. You have a page in your report that contains the following elements:
- Background Image: A shape containing a company logo or a relevant background image.
- Key Metrics: A card visual displaying a key performance indicator (KPI), such as total sales for the current quarter.
- Sales Trend: A line chart showing sales trends over time.
You want the "Key Metrics" card to be prominently displayed on top of the "Background Image" so it's the first thing users notice.
Question: What should you adjust in the Power BI report to ensure that the "Key Metrics" card appears in front of the "Background Image" shape?

  • A. Tab order in the Selection pane.
  • B. Layer order in the Selection pane.
  • C. Maintain layer order in the General visual settings.
  • D. Vertical alignment in the Canvas settings.
  • A. Tab order in the Selection pane.
  • B. Layer order in the Selection pane.
  • C. Maintain layer order in the General visual settings.
  • D. Vertical alignment in the Canvas settings.

Explanation: In Power BI, the Selection pane allows you to control the stacking or layer order of visuals on a report page. By adjusting the layer order, you can bring the "Key Metrics" card to the front, ensuring it appears on top of other elements like the background image. Tab order (A) controls keyboard navigation, not visual stacking, while vertical alignment (D) or General settings (C) affect positioning and sizing, not which visual appears in front.
