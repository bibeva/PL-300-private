Question 260

Imagine you're a Power BI developer at a bustling online retailer. You're building a dashboard to track sales performance, but you've noticed that different departments use various terms to refer to sales data.
The finance team uses "revenue," the marketing team prefers "turnover," and the sales team simply says "sales." You want to ensure everyone can easily query the data using their preferred terminology.
To enhance the Q&A experience, you propose adding synonyms for the "Sales" table in your dataset. These synonyms would include "revenue," "turnover," and other common terms used within the company.
Will this solution effectively improve the Q&A experience for users across different departments?

  • A. Yes
  • B. No, synonyms should be added for columns, not tables.
  • C. No, a linguistic schema is required for this scenario.
  • D. No, Q&A automatically recognizes these variations.
  • A. Yes
  • B. No, synonyms should be added for columns, not tables.
  • C. No, a linguistic schema is required for this scenario.
  • D. No, Q&A automatically recognizes these variations.

Explanation: Adding synonyms for a table (or its columns) in Power BI improves the Q&A experience by allowing users to ask questions using different terminology. In this scenario, mapping "revenue," "turnover," and "sales" as synonyms for the "Sales" table ensures that Q&A can understand queries from finance, marketing, and sales teams without confusion.
