Question 150

Imagine you're a financial analyst tracking monthly sales data for your company. You want to use Power BI to quickly identify any unusual spikes or dips in sales that might indicate unexpected trends or potential issues.
You know that Power BI has a built-in anomaly detection feature that can automatically detect such anomalies in your data.
Question: To enable the anomaly-detection capability and have Power BI automatically highlight unusual fluctuations in your monthly sales data, which visual and axis configuration should you choose?

  • A. Chart Type: Line chart
          Chart Configuration: Use a date field for the axis
  • B. Chart Type: Pie chart
          Chart Configuration: Enable cross-report drillthrough
  • C. Chart Type: Treemap
          Chart Configuration: Select the "Show value as" option
  • A. Chart Type: Line chart
          Chart Configuration: Use a date field for the axis
  • B. Chart Type: Pie chart
          Chart Configuration: Enable cross-report drillthrough
  • C. Chart Type: Treemap
          Chart Configuration: Select the "Show value as" option

Explanation: Power BI’s anomaly detection works with time series data, which requires a line chart with a date field on the axis. This setup allows Power BI to analyze trends over time and automatically identify unusual spikes or dips in monthly sales. Pie charts or treemaps do not support time-based anomaly detection.
