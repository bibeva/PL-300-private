Question 300

In your Power BI project, you are tasked with integrating customer information and order details into a unified dataset for analysis. You are working with two tables: CustomerInfo, which holds detailed information on customers, and OrderDetails, which records each customer's transactions.
Your objective is to consolidate these tables into a single, navigable dataset while ensuring no customer information is omitted, even if some customers have not placed orders. What action should you take to achieve this?

  • A. Generate a composite key.
  • B. Concatenate the queries for CustomerInfo and OrderDetails.
  • C. Activate "Assume Referential Integrity" option in Power BI.
  • D. Combine CustomerInfo with OrderDetails using a left-outer join.
  • A. Generate a composite key.
  • B. Concatenate the queries for CustomerInfo and OrderDetails.
  • C. Activate "Assume Referential Integrity" option in Power BI.
  • D. Combine CustomerInfo with OrderDetails using a left-outer join.

Explanation: A left-outer join ensures that all rows from the left table (CustomerInfo) are included in the resulting dataset, even if there are no matching rows in the right table (OrderDetails). This guarantees that customers without orders are still present in the unified dataset, which is essential for complete customer analysis. Concatenating queries or creating a composite key does not preserve unmatched customer records.
